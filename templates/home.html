Debutante

xx For me, I think it always starts with the music. It's always the music, the production- the tone and overarching feelings first. Like our everyday conversation, I don't think it REALLY matters what you say- but how you say it. Maybe honing it all the way down, tone is the key one I think. Anyways, I guess I was listening to a lot of fucking Nick Cave and The Wolfgang Press at the time and feeling very antsy about finally finishing school. I mean, for 5 years, every 4 months I move to a new city- that's just the reality of the lifestyle that my program affords. It really doesn't make building relationships or finding people to play these songs with very easy. Computer Science has forced me to be a very logical person; for example, I'm finding it increasingly difficult to find the motivation to talk small when everyone becomes a supernumerary under the taught practionality of it all. It's a shitty thing when you go to a party or a freak punk show in Ottawa or St. Catharines (which is where I was writing these songs), and you meet these interesting people but in the back of your mind, you're certain you're never going to have time for this person because of school. Ugh, fuck I just want to be done school so I can finally pull the people that I want into my life. Anyways, I want to play these songs. I want to make more things. I'm nearly done school. I want to be around creatives. Hit me up if you want to fuck shit up. - KS

Impressed by the golden era of post-punk from the late 70's and early 80s, if one would "Watch Closely" or rather, listen- influences can be traced back to Andy Gill's stringent guitar riffs, the violent fetishism of The Birthday Party and occassional delivery of The Wolfgang Press."
